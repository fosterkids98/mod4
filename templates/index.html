Past Events

The Battle of the Titans
Date:

2nd April, 85 AD

A grand contest between two legendary gladiators, each hailed as "The Titan" of their respective arenas. These two warriors, Marius and Octavian, have fought across the empire, and their final battle in the Colosseum was the most brutal yet. The fight began as a one-on-one duel but escalated when a group of armed gladiators were unleashed into the arena, turning it into a chaotic battle royale. The event was accompanied by spectacular displays of Roman war tactics and a final speech by the emperor, declaring Marius the victor, a true hero of the empire.

The Great Beast Exhibition
Date:

26th March, 85 AD

The Colosseum hosted an extravagant animal hunt featuring exotic creatures from the far reaches of the Roman Empire. The arena was filled with lions, tigers, and elephants, as well as the more unusual beasts like crocodiles and giraffes. Roman hunters and gladiators worked together in teams to subdue the beasts, demonstrating their skills in both combat and animal handling. The event was designed to show the might of the empire and its control over the natural world, thrilling the crowd with close encounters and daring hunts.

The Naval Battle of Alesia
Date:

20th March, 85 AD

In this extraordinary reenactment, the Colosseum was flooded to create a mock naval battle between Roman forces and the Carthaginian fleet. The Battle of Alesia was re-created on the water, with ships, rowers, and sailors all acting out the conflict. Fireballs and flaming arrows flew across the arena as mock sea battles unfolded, and Roman tactics were displayed in full. The spectacle ended with the symbolic victory of Rome, as the Roman ships closed in and "sank" the Carthaginian fleet. The event was one of the most ambitious ever held in the Colosseum.

The Triumph of Apollo: A Theatrical Performance
Date:

7th March, 85 AD

A grand theatrical production inside the Colosseum, blending dramatic storytelling with live performances and music. The story followed Apollo, the god of the sun and music, as he journeyed across the heavens and Earth to restore order in the mortal world. The play was filled with elaborate costumes, live music played by Roman musicians, and dramatic fight sequences between gods and mythical creatures. Gladiators participated in choreographed combat scenes, representing the struggle between order and chaos.
