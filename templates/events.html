15th April, 85 AD

Upcoming Events

Naval Battle Reenactment: The Battle of Actium
Date:

20th April, 85 AD

For this one-of-a-kind event, the Colosseum is flooded with water to simulate a naval battle. The reenactment of the famous Battle of Actium between Augustus and Mark Antony is brought to life with ships, sailors, and gladiators fighting in a mock sea battle. The crowds watch as ships clash in a dramatic fight for control of the seas. Real naval tactics are demonstrated, and spectators are treated to fireballs launched from warships.

The Triumph of Venus: A Grand Theatrical Play
Date:

30th April, 85 AD

This event is a grand theatrical performance featuring the goddess Venus and a cast of mythological figures. The play is staged in the Colosseum with elaborate costumes, stunning backdrops, and live music. The story depicts Venus journey from the heavens to Earth to resolve a dispute between the gods, with plenty of dramatic twists. Gladiators are also included in the performance, engaging in staged combat to represent the struggle between love and war.

The Mock Siege of Carthage
Date:

15th May, 85 AD

A simulation of the Roman victory over Carthage in the Third Punic War. The Colosseum is transformed into a miniature version of Carthage, complete with walls and gates. Gladiators and soldiers recreate the brutal siege, with catapults, battering rams, and siege towers used in mock combat. The event culminates in a dramatic capture of the "city" and the symbolic destruction of Carthage.
